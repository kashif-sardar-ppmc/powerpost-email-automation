02 July 2026:

Pakistan advances digital transformation via AI-driven youth initiatives and governance modernization, while fiscal federalism faces structural strain from underfunding and debt. 01 July 2026:

Pakistan’s economy grew 3.7% in FY2025-26, buoyed by export resilience and fiscal discipline, though it missed its IMF tax target by Rs975B. Energy markets saw mixed signals: stock gains driven by oil price dips and sectoral strength, while tariffs may rise in 2026 amid fuel cost pressures. Renewables, especially rooftop solar, are booming, yet policy remains focused on import reliance, hydropower trials, and energy security. Governance and regulatory gaps persist, including K-Electric’s leadership void and EV subsidy audits. Despite global tech and AI momentum, Pakistan’s energy and fiscal policy remain constrained by inflation, external shocks, and delayed green transition, with renewables sidelined amid fiscal and governance priorities.


30 June 2026:

Pakistan’s energy sector grapples with deep financial strain, circular debt, and volatile pricing amid IMF-backed reforms, while LNG imports face Strait of Hormuz disruptions. Renewables and climate adaptation lag, despite low emissions. Regulatory reforms face criticism, and reactive fuel price adjustments undermine stability. The PPSF aims to cushion consumers, yet operational clarity is lacking. Punjab’s electric bus rollout and China’s tech surge contrast with traditional sector decline.
